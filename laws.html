Laws of the North

The North is a land of harsh seasons and harsh justice. These laws reflect the traditions, honor, and survivalist values passed down by the kings and lords of Winterfell for generations.

I. Obedience to the Crown

  • The word of the King in the North is law. All bannermen and subjects owe their loyalty and obedience.
  • Decrees issued by the Winter Court shall carry the same authority as royal edicts.

II. Trial and Justice

  • All freefolk have the right to a trial, either by combat or before a sitting judge of the North.
  • Execution is carried out by the hand of the one who passes the sentence, in the old way.

III. Treason and Oathbreaking

  • Betrayal of the Crown, Court, or one's liege is punishable by death, exile, or forfeiture of lands.
  • False banners and desertion in war are considered capital offenses.

IV. Rights of Blood and Land

  • Heirs inherit by blood, and lands remain with rightful Northern houses unless taken by legal claim or conquest recognized by the Crown.
  • The Crown may redistribute lands in response to treason, rebellion, or bloodline extinction.

V. The Faith of the North

  • The worship of the Old Gods is held in highest esteem. Other faiths may be practiced, so long as they do not defy the laws or incite unrest.

VI. The King's Peace

  • Private war and feuding between houses is forbidden without royal sanction. The King's Peace must be honored at all times unless lifted by decree.

VII. The Law of Hospitality

  • Guest right is sacred. The violation of guest right—harming a guest or host after bread and salt—is considered a grave crime against the gods and realm.
